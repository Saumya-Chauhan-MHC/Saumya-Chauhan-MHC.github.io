EchoBlue

AI assistant for caregivers of children with autism

Project Overview

EchoBlue is a research‑driven mobile application designed to empower caregivers of children with autism. The app fuses video pose sequences and individualized education plan (IEP) documents in a dual‑encoder architecture to track developmental milestones, detect potential triggers and deliver personalised coaching. Inspired by our personal experiences and informed by work in autism assistive technology, EchoBlue aims to bridge the gap between data‑driven insights and day‑to‑day caregiving. Many existing apps for autism target medical staff and carers, providing short videos and external resources to help carers develop knowledge about autism[1]. EchoBlue builds on these foundations while bringing together AI models and caregiver feedback loops.

Key Features

  • Dual‑Encoder Model: A vision encoder processes pose sequences from videos, while a language encoder ingests IEP documents and caregiver annotations. Together they learn cross‑modal representations to identify behaviours, triggers and milestones.
  • Personalised Coaching: The app generates actionable guidance based on each child’s behavioural patterns and goals. AI‑powered mobile apps can be tailored to individuals’ specific learning difficulties, enabling personalised education and helping people with disabilities feel more connected and included in society[2].
  • Progress Tracking & Insights: Caregivers receive visualisations of developmental trajectories and are alerted when certain behaviours deviate from expected patterns. AI systems integrated within mobile apps can facilitate teaching procedures and increase concentration levels by analysing learners’ responses[2].
  • Collaborative Feedback Loop: Caregivers can annotate sessions, adjust goals and share observations. By analysing these responses, AI algorithms can discover insights into learning patterns and tailor future suggestions[3].
  • Community Resources: EchoBlue includes curated short videos, evidence‑based strategies and links to external resources, building on features common among current autism apps[1].

Impact & Future Directions

EchoBlue has already received validation from caregivers, clinicians and educators through interviews and pilot studies. As a co‑founder, I presented EchoBlue at the Bill Gross Entrepreneurship Competition—Caltech’s flagship business plan event where ten finalist teams (eight undergraduate, two graduate) pitch their ideas to industry judges[4]—and secured seed funding to continue development. Our next steps include clinical trials, expanding to other developmental conditions and open‑sourcing components of the model. By integrating novel AI methods such as progress tracking, personalised content delivery and automated reasoning, EchoBlue represents a step towards inclusive, human‑centred technology for autism care.
