Saumya Chauhan

I’m a recent B.S. in Computer Science (Caltech ’25) focused on interpretable multimodal learning, robust evaluation, and responsible AI. I build datasets, tools, and models that help people make better decisions—especially in high-stakes or social settings. Recent work spans legal AI interpretability (RuleSum), dynamic point tracking (ITTO), autism-support assistants (SHARE), and misinformation/GenAI diffusion on Reddit.

Research

I’m interested in: (1) safety, equity, and accountability in human–AI ecosystems and (2) interpretable assistive tools that augment human decision-making. Methodologically, I like exemplar-based interpretability, uncertainty-aware evaluation, and multimodal learning with careful, decision-level metrics. Representative papers are highlighted.
